Covid Forecast

Summary & Commentary

Summary: The nationwide surge seems to be abating, although some states (especially in the Midwest and Northeast) still seem to be increasing for now. The surge is likely a result of variants, relaxed health measures, and greater social mobility. As of writing, NYC is stable in viral spread & NYS is also stable

There is a 1-3 week lag for the forecast to show up in confirmed cases from testing (if the forecast has been accurate)

Disease Models

COVID-19 Forecast Hub
  • Ensemble model aggregates forecasts from 30+ models & is used by the CDC
CDC COVID-19 Forecasts
  • The United States Centers for Disease Control are the leading public health authority. They aggregate and publish frequent forecasts, all of which are listed at the link
Kinsa Healthweather
  • Kinsa monitors real-time data from millions of smart thermometers nationwide. May be an early warning system (assuming high signal-to-noise ratio)
YYG Covid Nowcast
  • AI-aided nowcasting of Covid infections (likely the most accurate one out there)
  • Also has a Path to Herd Immunity forecast
  • The author has published an enumerated list of prediction-evaluated models
538 Covid Model Guide
  • 538 offers a comprehensive guide about Covid models, including best practices for interpretation
View additional models
Google COVID-19 Model
  • A public machine learning model made by Google. Mileage may vary
IHME COVID-19 Model
  • COVID-19 Model made by The Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) at University of Washington. Mileage may vary
