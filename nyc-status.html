Relevant News

Updated weekly-ish. Last update: 12/14

Seasonal News
  • Two weeks ago, the U.S. observed the Thanksgiving holiday. There was a lot of domestic travel (though less than a non-pandemic year) and many social gatherings. Holiday activities likely are the result of significantly increased spread.
Micro-clusters & Covid Restrictions
  • New York revamped the Micro-cluster strategy â€“ targeted controls in virus hotspots â€“ for the winter to have a focus on hospital load-balancing, increased distributed testing, keeping schools open when safe, public education of small gathering spread, and implementing a vaccine program.
  • At time of writing, there are currently 26 micro-clusters across the state of New York. Specifically, New York City has 6 separate cluster zones. Updated clusters are expected on 12/14.
    • NYC Zones, in rough order of severity: entirety of Staten Island (both orange & yellow areas added on 11/25). Yellow cluster in Queens, which was expanded on 11/19. Bronx has two yellow zones covering large portions of the borough (added on 11/19). Manhattan yellow zone in the far upper region of the borough (Washington Heights), added on 11/25. Brooklyn has a shrinking yellow zone since 11/19.
High Risk Activities
  • As of 12/14, indoor dining is closed in NYC for the foreseeable future. Outdoor dining, takeout, and delivery will continue. This rule is expected to be continually reevaluated.
  • Bars and restaurants serving liquor are mandated by New York State law to close at 10pm. Gyms also have this 10pm closure rule.
  • Indoor gatherings are limited by law to a maximum of 10 people.
  • In Micro-cluster zones, there are additional restrictions on these and other activities. There is a low, but not insignificant, possibility of further restrictions such as 'NY on Pause', but it is to be used as an emergency brake if the cluster strategy falls through.
Vaccines
  • The Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine has been granted an emergency use authorization from the FDA. Distribution has already begun.
  • New York already administered the first dose and plans to vaccinate other first-priority people, after a key medical task force recommended it.
  • Moderna also filed for authorization with the FDA (potentially expected around 12/17). Both Pfizer & Moderna (so far) have outstanding effectiveness and safety results (around ~95% effective).
  • The earliest groups to get vaccines will likely include healthcare workers, long-term care residents, federal & essential workers, and elderly & immunocompromised people. States make the final decisions.
  • The Oxford vaccine may also prove to be an effective candidate. However, further trials are needed due to errors.
Schools
  • Public schools are currently going through a phased reopening after closing temporarily in November.
  • Since reopening, public schools have been at an astonishingly low ~0.3% positivity rate (which is seriously low and safer than practically anywhere else, if statistically sound).
  • There is emerging research supporting the reopening of college campuses, by analyzing universities that conducted mass testing in the New England region.
    • NYU's own results bolster this, as the school has stayed roughly around the 0.5% positivity mark so far (and always below 1.0%). It is important to note the caveat that the school in question should have appropriate policies and the student body likely has to have a culture of compliance.
Public Health News
  • 74% of all New York cases come from at-home gatherings, according to NYS contact tracing data from 12/11.
  • The virus is much more transmissible in colder, drier air.
  • The virus is much more transmissible indoors and in areas with poor ventilation and/or close contact.
  • There is some evidence that immunity after getting the virus may last at least 6-8 months. However, it is important to note that some people may get reinfected with the virus, despite already having it once (though it is rare).
  • Here's a running summary of recent relevant & important Covid findings, as reviewed by the scientific journal Nature.
  • Masks go a long way in protecting society and stopping spread. A Kansas study found that mask mandates successfully helped decrease Covid rates in compliant counties, while counties that opted out of mask mandates saw cases increase.
  • See here for detailed public health guidance & quick takeaways on how you can stay safe.
Covid Compliance
  • The conservative-leaning U.S. Supreme Court issued a controversial ruling against Governor Cuomo's imposition of certain capacity caps on religious gatherings. This is a complicated ruling and NYS may yet come up with different, more objective rules.
  • NYPD leadership told officers to mask up or face disciplinary action.
  • New York State recently announced a new privacy-minded Covid app, called COVID Alert NY. It keeps people safe using bluetooth iOS / Android technology (rather than tracking your location) & works regionally with apps in NY, NJ, PA, and DE. I urge you to consider downloading it.
