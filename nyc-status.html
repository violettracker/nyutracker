#1: New York State put out a new Micro-cluster strategy – targeted controls in virus hotspots, including some closures of primary schooling, nonessential businesses, religious institutions, dining, and bans on mass gatherings Between 10/10 – 10/12, 54,029 new tests were administered in NYC. Of these, 926 were positive, or 1.71%. New hospitalizations during this week remain low at 30 and there were 7 new deaths in the same period. The positive rate between 10/3 – 10/9 was 1.54% and the prior week was 1.77%. New York State recently announced a new privacy-minded Covid app, called COVID Alert NY. It keeps people safe using bluetooth iOS / Android technology (rather than tracking your location) & works regionally with apps in NY, NJ, PA, and DE. I urge you to consider downloading it New York State announced new restrictions & controls in virus hotspots, including some closures of primary schooling, nonessential businesses, religious institutions, dining, and bans on mass gatherings These clusters are mainly in Brooklyn and Queens, specifically in Southern Brooklyn, Williamsburg, Central Queens and Far Rockaway. Please exercise caution and use the preceding link to check up on your neighborhood. Cuomo recently announced that the state is sending hundreds of thousands of rapid test kits to schools in at-risk areas & also threatened to withhold state funds from localities that didn't properly enforce Covid restrictions. The state is enforcing Covid compliance through the use of State Police in clusters, and NYC is having some mask enforcement & fines through mask patrols. NYPD leadership recently told officers to mask up or face disciplinary action. Indoor dining opened on September 30th at 25% capacity and the fall season is forecasted by many experts to bring significant volatility, with possibly more spread in the coming weeks. Many schools reopened recently, which is inevitably bringing virus spread. However, thousands of NYC schools are also doing innovative things like holding class outside.
