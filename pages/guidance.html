Health Guidance

Quick Takeaways

VioletTracker is compiling the latest advice from trusted public health experts & authorities to deliver you a list of Covid best practices. You don’t have to do them all, but the more you do, the better off you will be (along with society)
  • Wear a mask (snug-fitting) with multiple layers or a filter if possible
  • Limit indoor gatherings; be outside instead
    • If you must socialize indoors, utilize a mask and/or strict social bubble, and properly ventilate
  • Avoid or limit high-risk activities, such as parties, bars, and indoor dining
    • To a slightly lesser extent, also gyms, hotels, personal care, religious institutions, and etc.
  • Avoid crowds, especially unmasked ones
  • Limit non-essential travel, especially inter-state or international
  • Socially distance (6 feet), wash your hands, and sanitize contact surfaces
  • If you suspect exposure, properly quarantine right away & get tested after about 3-7 days, or immediately if you show symptoms
  • Contact a medical professional and follow your local public health authority’s instructions, especially if symptoms worsen

VT Covid Protocol 1.0

Full public health guidance. You don’t have to do it all, but the more you do, the better off you will be (along with society)

Last major update: 12/03
Scan this list again if you haven't read since that date (this is a new disease and more research is coming out by the day)

  • Wear a mask
    •
    • Use a snug fitting face covering over your mouth and nose
    • Use cloth fabric for comfort, or disposable masks for convenience
    • In general, more layers = better mask; if possible, use a mask with a filter


  • Limit indoor gatherings
    •
    • Outdoors is inherently safer
    • If you're indoors, but with people not in your household, wear a mask
    • Otherwise, at least limit it to your bubble & ventilate (see below)
    • Limit the duration and/or frequency of gatherings


  • Avoid or limit being in high risk areas
    •
    • As much as possible, avoid: indoor parties & gatherings, bars, and indoor dining
    • To a slightly lesser extent: gyms, hotels, and other high risk places
    • Anywhere with unmasked people and/or poor ventilation is inherently high risk
    • Limit, if not avoid, non-essential travel (especially inter-state or international)
    • The more we collectively avoid high-risk things, the less restrictions there have to be


  • Socially distance
    •
    • When possible, maintain a 6 feet distance
    • Avoid crowds, especially unmasked ones
    • If you eat outside, try to do it where not many people are present or passing by


  • Ventilate
    •
    • Outdoor air circulation is good
    • If you have people over indoors, open a window and/or use an air purifier


  • Wash your hands
    •
    • Wash with soap whenever possible
    • Use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol
    • Disinfect items that have been exposed outside your home (be cautious rather than rigorous)
    • Disinfect items that other people have likely or possibly touched


  • Quarantine properly, when exposed
    •
    • If you suspect exposure, immediately quarantine
    • Do not leave your home; if you absolutely MUST to visit a doctor or etc., wear a mask when you go outside
    • Get tested about 3-7 days after you suspect exposure, or immediately if you notice symptoms
    • Contact a medical professional as soon as possible, especially if symptoms worsen
    • If you live with others, try to isolate yourself in your room and have someone deliver meals to your door (basically avoid person-to-person contact for at least 10 days)


  • Personal health
    •
    • Eat, sleep, and exercise in healthy ways to maintain your immune system
    • Get tested as often as possible, or as often as your local health authority advises
    • It may not be a bad idea to take Vitamin D supplements, as these may have some correlation with disease outcomes (though it is not definitively known)
    • Always follow public health guidance of your local health department & consensus of public health experts
    • There are a number of vaccines coming to life – do your research & be receptive to them once they're safely approved
    • Watch out for your mental health & utilize your support network


  • Utilize social bubbles, if you want an indoor social life
    •
    • If you choose to risk social gatherings, especially indoors, do it with a pre-defined social bubble
    • Note: All members of a social bubble should only be in that exclusive bubble, for adequate protection


  • Pro-tips (optional)
    •
    • It may be helpful, though not necessary, to wear 2 masks (e.g. a 1-layer mask under a 2-layer mask) – in the cold, it may even keep you warm
    • It may not be a bad idea to hold your breath when passing by unmasked people
    • If unable to hold your breath, exhale hard in your mask before inhaling for a makeshift air cushion
